PRISM: Wearable Hardware Platform

A modular wearable hardware system designed for real-time physiological data collection and experimentation.

Embedded SystemsHardwareSensors

Overview

PRISM is a modular wearable hardware platform built to give researchers a compact and flexible base for sensing experiments.

Key Focus

  • Reduce the friction of building wearable prototypes for new health sensing projects.
  • Combine the most useful board-level functions into one compact research platform.
  • Support fast iteration across different sensors, peripherals, and embedded workflows.

Methodology

  • The platform integrates an RTC, IMU, SD storage, battery charging, and modular GPIO or I2C connectivity.
  • It is designed to work with multiple microcontroller families through compatible board footprints.
  • Hardware choices prioritize data logging, low-friction prototyping, and straightforward expansion.

Key Outcomes

  • PRISM creates a reusable base for multiple wearable sensing experiments instead of one-off builds.
  • The board keeps cost and complexity lower while still supporting richer research setups.
  • It establishes a scalable platform for future student hardware projects and sensor studies.
